Major Agricultural Crops

Wheat

Growth: Cool season crop, sow in Oct-Nov, harvest in April-May

Major Regions: Punjab, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh

Optimal Soil: Well-drained loamy soil with pH 5.5-6.5

Key Fertilizers: NPK (120:60:40 kg/ha), Zinc (5 kg/ha)

Water Needs: Requires 4-6 irrigations, critical at crown root initiation stage

Common Varieties: HD 2967, PBW 343, DBW 17

Rice

Growth: Kharif crop, requires standing water, grown in monsoon

Major Regions: West Bengal, Uttar Pradesh, Andhra Pradesh

Optimal Soil: Clayey loam soils that retain water

Key Fertilizers: Urea (100-150 kg/ha), DAP (40-50 kg/ha)

Water Needs: Requires continuous water logging (5-10 cm)

Common Varieties: Pusa Basmati 1509, IR 64, Swarna

Corn (Maize)

Growth: Grown in both Kharif and Rabi seasons

Major Regions: Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Maharashtra

Optimal Soil: Well-drained fertile loam soil

Key Fertilizers: NPK (120:60:40 kg/ha)

Water Needs: Requires 4–5 irrigations

Common Varieties: HQPM-1, DHM 117, Bio-9637

Sugarcane

Growth: Tropical crop, 10–16 months duration

Major Regions: Uttar Pradesh, Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu

Optimal Soil: Deep, well-drained loam

Key Fertilizers: Nitrogen (150 kg/ha), Phosphorus (50 kg/ha)

Water Needs: Requires high water; 25–30 irrigations

Common Varieties: Co 0238, Co 86032

Cotton

Growth: Kharif crop, requires warm climate

Major Regions: Maharashtra, Gujarat, Telangana

Optimal Soil: Black soil with good moisture

Key Fertilizers: NPK (100:50:50 kg/ha)

Water Needs: Moderate; 3–4 irrigations

Common Varieties: Bt Cotton, Jayadhar

Soybean

Growth: Kharif crop, short duration (90–100 days)

Major Regions: Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Rajasthan

Optimal Soil: Well-drained loam and clay loam

Key Fertilizers: NPK (20:60:40 kg/ha)

Water Needs: Requires 3–5 irrigations

Common Varieties: JS 335, MAUS 61

Potato

Growth: Rabi crop in India, 90–120 days cycle

Major Regions: Uttar Pradesh, West Bengal, Punjab

Optimal Soil: Sandy loam rich in organic matter

Key Fertilizers: FYM, NPK (150:100:100 kg/ha)

Water Needs: Requires 5–6 irrigations

Common Varieties: Kufri Jyoti, Kufri Bahar

Mustard

Growth: Rabi oilseed crop

Major Regions: Rajasthan, Haryana, Madhya Pradesh

Optimal Soil: Well-drained loamy soils

Key Fertilizers: NPK (80:40:40 kg/ha)

Water Needs: 2–3 irrigations during flowering

Common Varieties: Varuna, Pusa Bold

Peanut (Groundnut)

Growth: Kharif & Summer crop

Major Regions: Gujarat, Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh

Optimal Soil: Sandy loam with good drainage

Key Fertilizers: Gypsum, NPK (20:40:40 kg/ha)

Water Needs: Moderate; 4–5 irrigations

Common Varieties: GG 20, TAG 24

Tea

Growth: Perennial crop; thrives in humid, hilly areas

Major Regions: Assam, West Bengal, Kerala

Optimal Soil: Acidic, well-drained soils

Key Fertilizers: Urea, Potash, Organic matter

Water Needs: High rainfall or irrigation

Common Varieties: Assamica, Cambod

Coffee

Growth: Tropical perennial crop

Major Regions: Karnataka, Kerala, Tamil Nadu

Optimal Soil: Deep, well-drained loamy soils rich in humus

Key Fertilizers: Nitrogen-rich compost and Potash

Water Needs: Regular rainfall or irrigation

Common Varieties: Arabica, Robusta

Millets

Growth: Hardy crops grown in dry regions

Major Regions: Rajasthan, Karnataka, Maharashtra

Optimal Soil: Well-drained sandy or loamy soil

Key Fertilizers: Low-input crop; requires compost and FYM

Water Needs: Drought-tolerant, minimal irrigation

Common Varieties: Bajra, Ragi, Jowar

Millets

Growth: Hardy crops grown in dry regions

Major Regions: Rajasthan, Karnataka, Maharashtra

Optimal Soil: Well-drained sandy or loamy soil

Key Fertilizers: Low-input crop; requires compost and FYM

Water Needs: Drought-tolerant, minimal irrigation

Common Varieties: Bajra, Ragi, Jowar

Barley

Growth: Rabi cereal crop, grown Nov–Apr

Major Regions: Punjab, Haryana, Rajasthan

Optimal Soil: Deep, well-drained loamy soil

Key Fertilizers: NPK (90:45:30 kg/ha)

Water Needs: 4–5 irrigations

Common Varieties: RD 2503, DWRB 73

Chickpea

Growth: Rabi pulse, sown Oct–Nov, harvested Mar–Apr

Major Regions: Madhya Pradesh, Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh

Optimal Soil: Light to medium loam

Key Fertilizers: NPK (20:60:40 kg/ha)

Water Needs: 3–4 irrigations

Common Varieties: JG 6, KAK 2

Lentil

Growth: Rabi pulse, short duration (~90 days)

Major Regions: Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, Madhya Pradesh

Optimal Soil: Sandy loam

Key Fertilizers: NPK (20:50:30 kg/ha)

Water Needs: 2–3 irrigations

Common Varieties: L 4147, Moitree

Sunflower

Growth: Kharif/Rabi oilseed crop, 90–120 days

Major Regions: Karnataka, Maharashtra, Rajasthan

Optimal Soil: Well-drained loamy soil

Key Fertilizers: NPK (60:40:20 kg/ha)

Water Needs: 4 irrigations

Common Varieties: KBSH 1, Morden

Coconut

Growth: Perennial tropical crop, ~60–80 fruits/tree/year

Major Regions: Kerala, Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh

Optimal Soil: Coastal sandy loam, pH 5.0–8.0

Key Fertilizers: NPK (160:60:180 g/palm/year)

Water Needs: Frequent irrigation, especially in dry months

Common Varieties: West Coast Tall, East Coast Tall

Rubber

Growth: Perennial tropical tree, tapped after ~6 years

Major Regions: Kerala, Tripura, Assam

Optimal Soil: Deep, well-drained loam with good organic matter

Key Fertilizers: N, P, K, Mg & micronutrients

Water Needs: High rainfall (>2000 mm/year)

Common Varieties: RRII 105, PB 86

Turmeric

Growth: Monsoon crop, harvested after 8–9 months

Major Regions: Kerala, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu

Optimal Soil: Well-drained loamy to red soil

Key Fertilizers: FYM, NPK (90:60:60 kg/ha)

Water Needs: Moderate; evenly moist soil

Common Varieties: Alleppey, Erode, Salem

Cardamom

Growth: Shade-loving spice crop in humid regions

Major Regions: Kerala, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu

Optimal Soil: Deep, loamy soils with pH 5.5–6.5

Key Fertilizers: FYM, NPK (120:70:70 kg/ha)

Water Needs: High humidity & rainfall

Common Varieties: Malabar, Mysore

Tomato

Growth: Grown year-round under irrigation, 90–120 days

Major Regions: Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka, Maharashtra

Optimal Soil: Well-drained loamy soils

Key Fertilizers: NPK (200:100:100 kg/ha)

Water Needs: Regular, 8–10 irrigations

Common Varieties: Pusa Ruby, Arka Rakshak

Brinjal

Growth: 120–150 days under warm conditions

Major Regions: West Bengal, Bihar, Maharashtra

Optimal Soil: Rich, well-drained loamy soil

Key Fertilizers: NPK (250:150:150 kg/ha)

Water Needs: 10–12 irrigations

Common Varieties: Pusa Purple Cluster, Arka Kusuma

Mango

Growth: Tropical perennial; bears fruit in summer (Mar–Jun)

Major Regions: Uttar Pradesh, Andhra Pradesh, Maharashtra

Optimal Soil: Deep, well-drained alluvial or laterite soil

Key Fertilizers: NPK (300:150:150 g/tree/year)

Water Needs: Seasonal; crucial in flowering stage

Common Varieties: Alphonso, Dasheri, Banganapalli

Banana

Growth: Year-round tropical fruit; cycle ~12 months

Major Regions: Tamil Nadu, Maharashtra, Gujarat

Optimal Soil: Rich, deep loamy with good drainage

Key Fertilizers: NPK (400:180:400 g/plant/year)

Water Needs: High moisture; frequent irrigation required

Common Varieties: Robusta, Grand Naine

Apple

Growth: Temperate fruit tree; 1200–1500 chill hours needed

Major Regions: Himachal Pradesh, Jammu & Kashmir

Optimal Soil: Well-drained loamy or sandy loam

Key Fertilizers: NPK (200:200:200 g/tree/year)

Water Needs: Regular irrigation in fruiting season

Common Varieties: Red Delicious, Royal Delicious

Grapes

Growth: Grown in warm, dry climate; harvest in winter

Major Regions: Maharashtra, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu

Optimal Soil: Deep, well-drained sandy loam

Key Fertilizers: NPK (200:100:200 kg/ha)

Water Needs: Moderate drip irrigation

Common Varieties: Thompson Seedless, Bangalore Blue

Agricultural Machinery

Tractor

Essential for plowing, hauling and operating implements

Types: Utility, Row-crop, Orchard, Compact

Power: 20-120 HP

Cost: ₹3 lakh - ₹15 lakh

Top Brands: Mahindra, TAFE, Sonalika, John Deere

Combine Harvester

Performs harvesting, threshing and cleaning in one operation

Types: Self-propelled, Pull-type

Top Brands: Dasmesh, Preet, John Deere

Power Tiller

Lightweight machine for small farms; used for tilling and puddling

Cost: ₹1.5 lakh - ₹3 lakh

Top Brands: VST, Kubota, KAMCO

Fertilizers & Soil Amendments

Urea (46-0-0)

Nitrogenous

Most widely used nitrogen fertilizer in agricultural soils

Composition:

46% Nitrogen

Application Rate:

50-150 kg/acre depending on crop

Best For:

Rice, Wheat, Maize, Cotton

Soil Types:

All except waterlogged conditions

Application Method:

Broadcast and incorporate before planting

Precautions:

Avoid direct contact with seeds

DAP (18-46-0)

Phosphatic

Highly concentrated phosphorus fertilizer with added nitrogen

Composition:

18% Nitrogen, 46% Phosphorus (P2O5)

Application Rate:

40-100 kg/acre

Best For:

Pulses, Cereals, Oilseeds

Soil Types:

Neutral to slightly acidic soils

Application Method:

Basal application before sowing

Precautions:

Do not mix with urea directly

Soil or foliar application

Precautions:

Do not mix with phosphate fertilizers

Borax

Micronutrient

Provides boron to aid flowering and fruit development

Composition:

10.5% Boron (B)

Application Rate:

1–2 kg/acre

Best For:

Sunflower, Cauliflower, Fruits

Soil Types:

Boron-deficient or sandy soils

Application Method:

Mix with sand and broadcast

Precautions:

Excess boron is toxic; apply carefully

Ferrous Sulphate

Micronutrient

Supplies iron for photosynthesis and chlorophyll synthesis

Composition:

19–20% Fe

Application Rate:

10–15 kg/acre

Best For:

Chlorotic crops, Acidic soils

Soil Types:

Iron-deficient soils

Application Method:

Soil application or foliar spray

Precautions:

Apply with chelating agents in alkaline soil

Potassium Nitrate (KNO₃)

Complex

Dual nutrient source for flowering and fruit development

Composition:

13% N, 46% K2O

Application Rate:

10–25 kg/acre

Best For:

Fruits, Vegetables, Flowers

Soil Types:

All well-drained soils

Application Method:

Foliar spray or fertigation

Precautions:

Use during flowering, not early stages

Government Agricultural Policies

National Agriculture Policy

Comprehensive framework for agricultural development with focus on sustainability, technology, and farmer welfare.

Key Objectives

  • 4% annual growth in agriculture sector
  • Efficient use of resources (water, soil, nutrients)
  • Promotion of organic farming practices
  • Development of agricultural infrastructure

Implementation Strategies

  • Soil Health Card Scheme
  • Neem Coated Urea
  • Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana
  • E-NAM electronic trading platform

Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN)

Direct income support scheme providing ₹6,000/year to small and marginal farmers.

Key Objectives

  • Provide assured income support
  • Reduce financial burden on farmers
  • Encourage investment in farming inputs

Implementation Strategies

  • ₹2,000 per installment (3 times/year)
  • Transferred directly to bank accounts
  • Beneficiaries identified via land records

Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)

Crop insurance scheme to protect farmers against crop loss due to natural calamities.

Key Objectives

  • Ensure financial support during crop failure
  • Stabilize farmer incomes
  • Encourage modern agriculture practices

Implementation Strategies

  • Premium: 2% for Kharif, 1.5% for Rabi
  • Widespread use of remote sensing and drones
  • Claims settled via Aadhaar-linked bank accounts

Kisan Credit Card (KCC)

Short-term credit scheme offering low-interest loans to meet crop and allied needs.

Key Objectives

  • Provide easy and timely credit access
  • Cover production and consumption needs
  • Reduce dependency on informal moneylenders

Implementation Strategies

  • Loan limit up to ₹3 lakh
  • Interest subvention up to 3%
  • Credit via commercial & cooperative banks

Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY)

Promotes organic farming through cluster-based approach and farmer certification.

Key Objectives

  • Encourage chemical-free farming

  • Promote group organic certification

Implementation Strategies
